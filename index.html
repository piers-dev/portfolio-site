Bratus Saltus

Bratus Saltus is a fast-paced speedrunning platformer about using special 'seeds' with interesting properties. The game sees you using your vine whip to create platforms at will and teleport around levels, all for a better time.

The game was developed for the Global Game Jam 2023. I was the gameplay programmer, graphics programmer, and level designer for our team of three.

Death to Die

Death to Die is cozy-yet-violent RPG about fighting off hoards of polyhedral enemies using your elemental powers. The game was developed for the GMTK Game Jam 2022.

I was responsible for everything in-engine, including code, particles, and most shaders. I also modelled the main character and made a lot of the animations that can be seen in-game.

Cat Walk Simulator

Cat Walk Simulator is a hilarious, lighthearted bullet-hell game about defending your meandering cat from an onslaught of angry neighbours. The game was developed for the GMTK Game Jam 2021.

I created all of the code, effects, levels, and UI for the game. I also designed most of the characters, and oversaw the integration of assets from the other artist I was working with.

Oh god TOO FAST

Oh god TOO FAST is an intense high-score game about piloting a space ship through fields of debris. The twist is that your ship needs fuel only to brake and turn, so steering it away from hazards can be quite the challenge. The game was developed for the GMTK Game Jam 2020.

I made all of the code, effects, and UI for the game. This includes the infinite level generation and gravity physics. I was also responsible for implementing art and music from my teammates.

MURDLE

MURDLE is a fast-paced multiplayer reinterpretation of the Wordle concept. It puts your vocabulary to the test by pitting you against your friends in quick matches of Wordle-like guessing. Every player would first come up with an obscure five letter word. Once everyone had picked, it became a speed-game of trying to guess your opponents' words simultaneously. If your word was revealed, you're out.

I developed MURDLE on my own to test my programming and design skills. I produced all of the code and art in the final version. Unfortunately it's no longer playable online, but screenshots can be found in the Gallery.
